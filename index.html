Why the Status Quo Doesn't Work

Six problems that AI-augmented delivery solves.

1.Enterprise SaaS — the game has changed

AI-augmented delivery on open-source foundations now makes it faster and cheaper to build a solution that fits the business exactly — with AI process automation built in — than to bend a generic platform into shape with expensive specialist consultants. The economics that justified the SaaS trade-off no longer hold for new initiatives.

2.Big Consulting & SIs — cost without capability

Large consulting firms charge premium rates but deliver slowly. Engagements balloon in scope and cost, staffed by rotating teams of junior developers. When the engagement ends, institutional knowledge walks out the door. The client is left dependent on the next expensive engagement.

3.Low-Code dead end — AI can't drag-and-drop

AI writes, reads, and reasons about code natively. It cannot drag and drop or manipulate visual designers. The very abstractions that made low-code "easy" for humans have become a barrier for AI-assisted development — without the vendor lock-in, licensing costs, or ceiling on customization.

4.The AI execution gap — pilots that never scale

Most enterprise AI initiatives stall as pilots that never reach production. There's a vast gap between "we use ChatGPT for emails" and "AI is fundamentally transforming how we deliver software." Organizations lack a credible, repeatable path from AI ambition to measurable business outcomes.

5.Big Agile regression — ceremony replaced rigor

Two decades of enterprise "Agile" adoption paradoxically degraded the SDLC discipline it promised to improve. Requirements analysis, architecture, specifications, and documentation — replaced with endless loosely-defined sprints and ad-hoc coding. No traceability, inconsistent quality, no institutional memory.

6.The initiative bottleneck — backlog grows, nothing ships

When every project requires a six-figure consulting engagement or a twelve-month internal build, only the highest-priority initiatives survive the business case. The rest sit in a queue, accumulating opportunity cost. The organization knows what needs fixing but can't afford to fix it all.

    display_field :name
    nav do icon "hero-cube"; order 10 end

      columns [:name, :status, :price]
    end

      section "Product Info" do
        fields [:name, :description, :price, :status]
      end
    end
    form do
      section "Product Info" do
        fields [:name, :description, :price]
      end
    end
  end
end
